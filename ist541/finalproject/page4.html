Warming Up Before a Run

Exercising cold muscles can lead to injury, so warm up your body before you start running. Begin with a few minutes of walking, then transition to dynamic stretching. Dynamic stretches work well before a run because they ease the body into movement.

Check out REI's advice on dynamic vs static stretching: trail running stretches


Cooling Down After a Run

Just as you need to warm up your muscles prior to running, it is equally important to stretch after you finish a run. Completing a gentle stretching routine that includes all major muscle groups after a run can loosen tight muscles and improve flexibility.
