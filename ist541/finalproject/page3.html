Preparing for a Run

Step 1: Check the Weather

These factors will affect your choice of route, clothing, distance, and pace:

  • Temperature
  • Wind
  • UV index
  • Humidity
  • Cloud cover
  • Air quality


It is helpful to know what pollutants are in the air. If you are sensitive to certain allergens, then high pollen or dust counts may affect your run.

Step 2: Pick a Route

Decide how far or how long you want to run, then select a route that supports your goal. Other considerations include:

sunny Weather

  • Hot? Choose a route with shade.
  • Windy? Select an out-and-back route so you can start running into the wind and finish with the wind at your back.

terrain Terrain

  • Need to condition yourself for elevation gain? Choose a route with hills.
  • Need to run speed intervals? Find a synthetic track.

favorite Personal Preferences

  • Get bored with repetion? Map out a loop.
  • Want a break from the noise of the city? Find an open space park.

Step 3: Select Your Attire

Select your clothing based on the weather and trail surface.

sunny Hot Weather

  • Hat for sun protection
  • Tank top or short sleeve shirt
  • Shorts
  • Lightweight synthetic socks

ac_unit Cold Weather

  • Beanie or warm headband
  • Long sleeve insulated shirt
  • Tights
  • Heavy synthetic/wool blend socks

terrain Trail Surface

  • Trail shoes with grippy tread for dirt or rocky paths
  • Road shoes with traditional tread for paved paths

Step 4: Gather Support Tools

Select your support tools based on the weather, duration of your run, and personal preferences.

sunny Weather: If it's sunny, you'll want dark tinted sunglasses. If it's cloudy, lighter tint sunglasses will reduce glare without limiting your vision.

schedule Duration: For longer runs, you may need a hydration pack. This is especially important on hot days or if your route does not include a water source.

favorite Personal Preferences: Wear a digital sport watch to track your time, distance, and/or pace. Bring headphones if you prefer listening to a guided run, podcast, or music.


sunglasses, sport watch, ear buds, and hydration pack


Step 5: Warm Up

Walk for 5-10 minutes to warm up your muscles and joints. Then do some dynamic stretching to activate the major muscle groups. Begin your run with an easy effort, and then slowly increase your effort over the course of the run.


warning
Remember to tell someone where you are going and
when you expect to return, especially if running alone.
